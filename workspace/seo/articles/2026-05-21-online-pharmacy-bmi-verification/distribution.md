---

Online pharmacies have a verification problem they did not sign up for.

For years, BMI eligibility checks for weight-loss prescribing have run on the same flow: the patient types in their weight, uploads one or two photos, a clinician glances at the file, and the order moves forward.

That flow assumed good-faith patient behaviour and reviewer time. Neither holds now.

📱 In 2026, any patient with a phone and a free AI account can generate a convincing inflated "before" body image in seconds. UK clinical teams have started flagging photo manipulation as a recurring pattern in real ordering flows, not an edge case.

The structural problem is not the patient. It is the verification method.

A camera-roll upload has no provenance:
→ no capture time
→ no liveness
→ no proof the photo belongs to the patient submitting it

When a prescribing decision for a regulated medication depends on what sits in an uploaded file, the audit defence "we looked at the photo" no longer holds.

What real BMI verification needs to look like in 2026:
✅ Live, in-session photo capture (not camera roll)
✅ Liveness and pose checks at the moment of capture
✅ Clothing detection to flag oversized fit
✅ AI-derived weight estimate cross-checked against self-report
✅ Audit-ready, exportable evidence

This is what we built FitXpress for. A leading UK online pharmacy already runs it as their BMI verification step inside checkout. UK GPhC scrutiny of online weight-loss prescribing is rising. US FDA monitoring of telehealth weight-loss pathways is rising too.

### Variant A — Procurement checklist angle

**Suggested profile:** linkedin-company (or Vadim Bilan if you want a practitioner-voice cut)

---

If you are picking a body-data vendor for an online pharmacy verification step, this is the procurement list.

The point is to separate a serious verification vendor from a photo-handling tool with a body data feature:

✅ Is photo capture in-session through your SDK, or can the patient upload from camera roll?
✅ Do you run liveness checks at capture, or only quality checks on the resulting image?
✅ How do you flag oversized clothing and posture-based BMI inflation?
✅ Do you produce an independent weight or body estimate and cross-check it against the patient's self-report?
✅ What does your audit record contain, and can you export it for regulator review without screenshots?
✅ Where is data stored, how long, and under what encryption?
✅ Will you sign a Business Associate Agreement (US HIPAA) and confirm GDPR alignment (UK/EU)?
✅ Can verification run server-side so body metrics are not exposed back to the patient?

If a vendor cannot answer the first two cleanly, the rest does not matter. In-session capture and liveness are the floor.
