Our CEO Katerina tested what's been on many online pharmacy teams' minds. She asked ChatGPT and Gemini to make her look 27 kg heavier in a photo. Both did it in seconds. Either output could pass a quick clinical glance.

That's the gap in photo-upload BMI checks. A patient submits a photo from their camera roll. There's no way to know when it was taken, whether it's been edited, or if it's even the same person. UK pharmacy teams say they're seeing this in real ordering flows for weight-loss medication.

FitXpress closes that gap. Photos are captured inside the session through the SDK, not from the camera roll. Liveness and pose checks run in real time. A clothing detector flags oversized attire. Smart Scales cross-checks the self-reported weight against the AI-derived body estimate. Results in under 45 seconds: BMI plus 80+ measurements. A leading UK online pharmacy uses it as their verification step today.

We wrote up what a defensible standard looks like in 2026 and what to ask any vendor claiming to solve this.
