Our CEO tested it herself. She asked ChatGPT to generate a photo of her looking 27 kg heavier. It took seconds. Face stayed real, body changed. Gemini produced a different version, equally convincing. Both could pass a quick clinical review.

This is not a future risk. It's what any patient with a free account can do before submitting a BMI photo today.

In Israel, digital health is moving fast. Telehealth platforms, wellness apps, insurtech players — all adding eligibility screening to their workflows. BMI verification is one of the most common checkpoints. Most still rely on a camera-roll upload.

That method was never built for this.

A file upload has no provenance. You don't know when it was taken, who was in the frame, or whether it's a real photo or an AI-generated one. The upload model stops being a verification step. It becomes a formality.

What closes the gap: in-session capture (not from the camera roll), liveness checks, clothing detection, a body estimate cross-checked against self-reported weight, and an exportable audit trail.

FitXpress does this in under 45 seconds — 80+ body measurements, BMI derived from the scan, and a mismatch flag when numbers don't line up. A leading UK online pharmacy already runs it as their BMI verification step.
