## Anti-positioning — what NOT to lead with

| Don't say | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "Most accurate body scanning" | Sets up loss when Apple/Google ships native primitives. Lead with workflow + outcomes instead. |
| "AI-powered body scanning" (alone) | Generic, AI-buzzword saturated. Always pair with concrete outcome. |
| "Replace your in-person fitting" | Some customers still need in-person — better to say "remove unnecessary fittings" |
| "Disrupt body measurement" | We don't disrupt; we deepen the workflow. Banned word per CLAUDE.md no-go. |
| "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary" | Banned per CLAUDE.md no-go. |
| "Just plug in our API" | Undersells the workflow value; sounds commodity. |

## Outbound subject line patterns

For email or LinkedIn first message — short, specific, no hype.

Good patterns:
- "Re: [their pain] — [our outcome]" — e.g., "Re: GLP-1 retention — verified progress data"
- "[Specific KPI improvement] for [their company type]" — e.g., "Faster pre-auth at bariatric clinics"
- Question form (use sparingly): "How is [Company] handling [specific pain]?"

Bad patterns (avoid):
- "Quick question" — generic, low signal
- "Touching base" — empty calorie
- "Are you the right person?" — burns goodwill

## Tone calibrations by audience

| Audience | Tone | Length |
|----------|------|--------|
| C-level (insurance, telehealth) | Formal, data-driven, business-outcome focus | Shorter |
| VP Engineering / Product | Technical, integration-focused, less business jargon | Medium |
| Founders (MTM brands, small clinics) | Direct, peer-to-peer, founder voice | Shorter |
| Heads of Compliance | Conservative, careful, compliance-forward | Medium-long with specific cert callouts |
| CROs / clinical operators | Process-oriented, audit-ready language | Medium-long |

## Forbidden in messaging (banned words list)

Per CLAUDE.md, NEVER use in any message, post, or article:

- leverage
- utilize
- harness
- robust
- seamless
- comprehensive
- delve
- navigate (in metaphorical sense)
- tapestry
- realm
- "It's not just X, it's Y" construction
- Em-dash in rhetorical constructions
- "Furthermore / Moreover / Additionally" at the start of sentences (minimize)
