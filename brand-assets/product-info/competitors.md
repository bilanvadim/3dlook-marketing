# Competitive Landscape (3DLOOK)

## Direct competitors

### 1. Prism Labs (primary FitXpress competitor)
**Who they are:** Mobile body composition scanning, focused on health at scale.

**Their primary bets:**
- Weight loss & GLP-1 programs (body composition tracking, lean-mass / muscle preservation monitoring)
- Insurance & population health (risk stratification, member incentives, body comp beyond BMI)
- Chronic disease management (metabolic syndrome, diabetes prevention, cardiovascular risk via visceral fat)
- Fitness & wellness platforms (lead-magnet scans, progress tracking, personalized programming)

**Strengths:**
- Strong narrative around "body composition beyond BMI"
- Established in insurance / population health
- Deep relationships in clinical settings

**Weaknesses (relative to us):**
- Less developed in MTM / apparel — they don't have Mobile Tailor equivalent
- Less white-label flexibility (smaller customizable surface area)

**How we differentiate:**
- Our 9+ years of training data and accuracy benchmarks
- Workflow-layer positioning (audit logs, integration depth)
- Two-product portfolio (FX + MT) — broader applicability for diversified customers

**When we lose to them:** customers laser-focused on clinical body comp metrics (visceral fat, etc.) where Prism has built specific clinical narrative.

---

### 2. Bodygram
**Who they are:** Mobile body measurement primarily for fitness / wellness audiences.

**Their primary bets:**
- Fitness trainers & gyms (longitudinal progress tracking)
- Health professionals (remote patient measurements, time-series comparisons)
- Dieticians & nutritionists (real-time scanning for individualized advice)

**Strengths:**
- Simple, clean trainer-focused UX
- Reasonably priced for SMB / solo professionals

**Weaknesses (relative to us):**
- Weaker clinical / enterprise workflow capabilities
- Less stringent compliance posture (no strong HIPAA narrative)
- No apparel / MTM business

**How we differentiate:**
- Enterprise-grade compliance (HIPAA, GDPR, audit logs)
- Verified body data (Smart Scales mismatch detection) for fraud / eligibility — Bodygram doesn't have this
- White-label depth

**When we lose to them:** SMB / single-trainer / boutique gym deals where price is the dominant factor.

---

### 3. Size Stream
**Who they are:** Smartphone + hardware-based body composition for clinical journeys.

**Their primary bets:**
- Smartphone-based body composition for clinical workflows (intake, treatment analysis, whole health-journey)
- Treatment response tracking (incl. weight-loss drug adoption)
- Research & hybrid monitoring (on-prem + at-home)

**Strengths:**
- Hybrid model (on-prem hardware + at-home smartphone)
- Strong in research / academic networks

**Weaknesses (relative to us):**
- Hardware dependency makes scaling expensive for customer
- Less consumer-grade UX
- Smaller commercial footprint

**How we differentiate:**
- Pure smartphone, no hardware needed
- Faster integration (API + SDK in days, not months)
- Better consumer-facing UX (real-time pose detection, AI clothing detector, face obfuscation)

**When we lose to them:** customers with existing on-prem hardware investments who want incremental at-home scanning.

## Long-term strategic risk: Apple / Google native primitives

**Risk:** OS vendors may expose body / pose / measurement primitives at the platform level (similar to ARKit, Vision framework). This could compress margins and make some "good enough" scanning use cases easy to replace.

**How we turn this into advantage:**
- We never lead with "best model" — we lead with **outcomes + workflow + governance**
- Apple/Google won't ship workflow integrations, audit logs, customer SLAs, on-device privacy modes, drift monitoring — these are our moat
- We invest in **trusted workflow layer**: longitudinal tracking, consent/permissions, audit logs, deep integration that's costly to swap

**Messaging implication for outbound and content:**
- Avoid statements like "the most accurate scanning available" — undermines this defense
- Prefer "trusted infrastructure for verified body data in regulated workflows"

## How agents should use this

- **hypothesis-generator:** when building a new hypothesis, check if Prism Labs / Bodygram / Size Stream are dominant in that vertical. If yes, sharpen our hypothesis to where we have a moat (compliance, two-product breadth, white-label depth, workflow integration).
- **seo-outline-builder:** for comparison content, follow `our-product` vs each competitor honestly. Don't trash competitors — strong, factual differentiation wins.
- **message-sequencer:** never name competitors in cold outbound. If prospect names a competitor in reply, then talk about us vs them in `response-classifier` flow.
