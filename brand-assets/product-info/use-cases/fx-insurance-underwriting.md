# Use Case: FitXpress — Insurance Underwriting

## The pain we remove
Underwriting still depends on self-reported body metrics + manual verification, driving delays, drop-offs, and weak documentation.

## Why now
Underwriting is going digital, but verification is the bottleneck. Insurers want no-medical-exam underwriting tiers but need defensible inputs.

## What FitXpress is here
2-photo scan inside the application — captures objective body metrics + BMI/body composition (structured, time-stamped, audit-ready).

## Hero message
Verify body metrics remotely to issue faster, cut rework, and strengthen auditability.

## ICP
Life & disability insurers, group benefits carriers, digital insurance platforms, reinsurers.
**Buyers:** VP Underwriting, CUO, Head of Digital Innovation, VP New Business, CRO.

## Examples to target
**US:** Prudential, MetLife, Guardian Life, New York Life, Lincoln Financial.
**EU:** Aviva (UK), Legal & General (UK), Allianz (DE), AXA (FR), Generali (IT).

## KPIs we improve
- Underwriting cycle time (days)
- Application completion / conversion rate
- Discrepancy and rework rate
- Audit readiness (consistent, time-stamped records)

## Market sizing
**TAM:** $25-75M / year (50-150M events × ~$0.50/scan)
**SAM (US + EU/UK):** $5-15M / year

## Commercial value
Lower ops cost + higher conversion from less friction + fewer manual checks.

## AI resilience
We sell defensible underwriting workflows (governance + audit logs + integration), not "perfect measurements." OS-level features won't replace the workflow layer.

## Critical messaging
- Lead with audit-readiness and compliance posture
- HIPAA mention essential (US insurance is HIPAA-adjacent)
- Specific GDPR mention for EU
- Reference time-stamped records, structured exports
- Do NOT lead with "best accuracy"

## Anti-pattern
Don't pitch insurance as "fitness tech." This is enterprise risk infrastructure.
