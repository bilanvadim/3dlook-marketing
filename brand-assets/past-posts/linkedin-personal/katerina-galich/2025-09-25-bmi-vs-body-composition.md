Can we talk about BMI for a second?

That one number has been used for decades to define "healthy weight," but it barely scratches the surface.

BMI doesn't know if you're a bodybuilder with 10% body fat or if you carry extra fat around your waist – it just sees weight vs. height.

In modern health and wellness, body composition is the real game-changer.

Two people with the same BMI can have dramatically different health profiles: one might have high body fat and low muscle, another might be lean and muscular.

So why are we still obsessing over BMI charts?

New tech makes it easier than ever to measure what truly matters: body fat percentage, muscle mass, visceral fat, hydration levels – the whole picture.

By focusing on these metrics, doctors and coaches can tailor advice much more effectively (and we as individuals get a more honest view of our progress).

The next time someone says your health is a single number, remember you're more complex than that – and now we have the tools to capture it.
