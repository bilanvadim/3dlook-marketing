In the past few weeks, I've had the same conversation with multiple online pharmacy teams across the UK.

Patients are using AI to manipulate photos before submitting them for BMI verification.

Not occasionally. Regularly enough that clinical teams are flagging it as a real problem.

The issue isn't just fraud. It's that doctors can no longer rely on what they're seeing. A photo that looks real may not be. And when prescribing decisions depend on BMI eligibility, that gap between appearance and reality creates direct clinical risk.

This is what happens when verification is built on a process that was never designed to be gamed and now is.

Live photo upload stops fake or altered photos. Clothing detection flags baggy attire used to inflate visual BMI. AI-generated weight and body data replaces subjective photo review entirely.

We built FitXpress specifically for this.
