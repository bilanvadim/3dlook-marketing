Use the same tech used to fake data, to catch fake data.

BMI checks aren't just a GLP-1 thing. Pharmacies use them for prescribing eligibility, and hospitals/clinics use them too: surgery pathways, anaesthesia risk, pre-op criteria. BMI is one of those gatekeeping inputs that quietly sits inside serious decisions.

And the weak link is often the same: photo uploads.

The problem with "upload two photos" isn't only that AI can edit them. It's that an upload has no provenance. You don't know when it was taken, if it's old, if it's someone else, if it's been manipulated, or if it's a photo of a photo. You're asking for "evidence" via a file attachment and hoping everyone behaves.

That's not a patient problem. It's a verification method problem.

The uncomfortable truth: AI has made fake evidence cheap.

So the answer isn't "ban AI". It's use AI to force reality back into the workflow:
✓ AI-powered live photo capture (not camera roll): images taken in-session, inside the verification flow
✓ Liveness checks: ensure it's a real person, not a printed photo or screen
✓ Fraud/manipulation detection: oversized clothing, suspicious volume inflation, unnatural shapes
✓ Real-time quality guardrails: pose, distance, angle checks without turning it into a video call
✓ Regulatory-ready evidence: timestamps, what was captured, pass/fail outcomes, exportable logs. Not screenshots.

AI can generate fake bodies in seconds. It can also be the layer that ensures your data came from a real human, in real time, in a way you can audit.

If your eligibility gate is a camera-roll upload, you're not verifying BMI. You're collecting content.
