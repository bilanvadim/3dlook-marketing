I ran an experiment that kept me up at night.
I asked ChatGPT and Gemini to generate photos of me 27 kg heavier (63 kg actual → 90 kg prompted).

ChatGPT made me visibly wider but kept my face unchanged easy to combine with a real headshot.
Gemini produced a more anatomically accurate body, but altered my face entirely, still convincing enough to fool a glance.
Both took seconds. Both could pass as real.

Here's what that means: doctors are prescribing GLP-1 medications right now based on "before" photos that may never have existed.

If I can create convincing fake BMI baseline photos in 90 seconds with free tools, what's actually happening at scale across telehealth platforms?

This isn't a workflow problem. It's a clinical verification problem.
Photo-based BMI verification was never designed for this. It needs to move beyond images - live photo upload, real measurements, uneditable data.
Healthcare deserves better.
