## How 3D Body Measurements Solve Limitations in Manual Measurements

Directly linked to measurers' skills and judgments, manual measurements can often lead to errors and variations. Additionally, different postures, compressed soft tissue from the measuring tape, and fatigue in the person being measured can also affect the outcome.

The human body's soft and elastic nature makes it difficult to measure accurately with a traditional tape measure.

Factors like breathing and muscle contraction can lead to different measurement results even for the same person. For instance, the variance in inhalation and exhalation can lead to significant differences in bust measurements. Muscle contractions, especially when arms are extended, impact arm length measurements.

Furthermore, individuals may adopt different standing postures, such as sticking out their chests when being measured.

With tape measuring, it's also unlikely to achieve a perfect horizontal outline.

All of the above pose no challenge for 3D body measurements.

## What is mobile body scanning technology?

Mobile body scanning technology uses a variety of sensors, such as cameras and depth sensors combined with AI technologies and 3D to create a 3D model of your body. This model can then be used to measure your body dimensions with high accuracy.

## How does mobile body scanning work?

To use mobile body scanning technology, a smartphone or tablet with a camera and depth sensor is needed. Users can then download a mobile body scanning app or use web-based solutions like Mobile Tailor, and follow the instructions to create a 3D model of their body.

Once their 3D model is created, the AI measures body dimensions. For example, it can measure height, weight, bust, waist, hips, and inseam.

Mobile Tailor generates over 80 precise body measurements. Businesses can also use the 3D model to create custom-fitted clothing or track your fitness progress.
